№ 02 · The story

A florist's second floor & a curated mess.

Napasorn has been at the Bangkok flower market for more than twenty years, arranging blooms for the front-of-house of the old town from the ground floor of 67 Chakkraphet Road. Mr Phuwanart Chumsrikarin and his team run the bouquet end of the business. The cafe upstairs is his own — a private hobby he opened to the public.

"Floral Café belongs to the kind of café you enter the way you might step into a storybook — a quiet, fragrant escape where flowers spill from every corner." — The Drink Journal, Nicholas Lin · Dec 2024

Walk in past the dried flowers and curiosities of the florist's nook. Take the narrow crooked stairway up. Emerge into a room of crystal chandeliers, framed portraits, oversized mirrors and what looks like straw bunga manggar hanging from the ceiling. The brick walls are white-washed. The sunlight is reliable. The cake stand is full.

The kitchen, quietly, doesn't do everything itself. The daily cakes arrive fresh from Bite Me Softly — the patisserie partner. The Macadamia Cheesecake is the regulars' standing order. The Lemon Curd Cheesecake is the runner-up. The Lavender Rose tea is the photograph everyone takes.

№ 03 · A three-storey daydream

Florist · cafe · daydream.

The building is read from bottom to top — start with the bouquets, climb the staircase, finish in the room that's still being plotted. The brand calls itself a three-storey daydream and we kept the count honest.

01Ground · The Florist

Napasorn · 20+ years on the street.

A florist's nook full of dried flowers, vases and the ebullient country-bohemian arrangements Mr Phuwanart's team is known for. Walk in and walk past — the cake comes upstairs.

02Second · The Cafe

The room · a curated mess of vintage.

Crystal chandeliers, antique candelabra, framed portraits, rattan chairs, cake stands and tall windows. The cake plate sits beside the bouquet. The flowers find their way into the tea.

03Third · The Daydream

The room · still being plotted.

A staircase past the cafe leads up to what was once a private collector's room. Mr Phuwanart told Siam2nite he'd one day open a restaurant up there. We're waiting.
